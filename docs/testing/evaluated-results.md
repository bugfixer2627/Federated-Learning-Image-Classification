
# Evaluation Results

## Overview

The project evaluates federated learning performance under multiple experimental conditions.

Testing focused on:

- Accuracy
- Reliability
- Privacy
- Robustness
- Availability

---

## Test Categories

### Baseline Federated Learning

Objective:

Evaluate standard federated learning performance without additional privacy or robustness mechanisms.

Metrics:

- Model Accuracy
- Loss
- Training Stability

---

### Privacy Evaluation

Objective:

Measure the impact of privacy-preserving mechanisms such as homomorphic encryption.

Metrics:

- Accuracy Retention
- Computational Overhead
- Communication Overhead

---

### Robustness Evaluation

Objective:

Evaluate model resilience against malicious participants.

Metrics:

- Accuracy Under Attack
- Aggregation Stability
- Defense Effectiveness

---

### Availability Evaluation

Objective:

Evaluate training continuity when clients disconnect during training.

Metrics:

- Model Accuracy
- Training Completion Rate
- System Reliability

## Key Findings

The project demonstrates that:

- Federated learning can train models without sharing raw user data.
- Privacy-preserving mechanisms can be integrated into the training workflow.
- Robustness defenses can mitigate malicious client behavior.
- Training can continue under partial client participation.
